In Cryptography, a Caesar Cipher is one of the simplest and most widely known encryption techniques. The method is named after Julius Caesar, who used it in his private correspondence.

How to Solve

To solve a Ceasar Cipher, you should shift the alphabet based on a given value. You may have to try every value to brute force it.
For example, if our key is 5, we will shift the alphabet by 5.
abcdefghijklmnopqrstuvwxyz
fghijklmnopqrstuvwxyzabcde
Now F corresponds to A, G corresponds to B, and so on. Now we can decode shifted messages, or code our own.
