Vigenere Cipher

In Cryptography, a Vigenere Cipher is a method of encrypting text by using a series of interwoven ceasar ciphers.

How to Solve

To decrypt, take the first letter of the ciphertext and the first letter of the key, and subtract their value (letters have a value equals to their position in the alphabet starting from 0). If the result is negative, add 26 (26=the number of letters in the alphabet), the result gives the rank of the plain letter.

For example, if your key is APPLE, you repeat it across the alphabet as follows:
abcdefghigklmnopkrstuvwxyz
APPLEAPPLEAPPLEAPPLEAPPLEA
This is your guide to how much you rotate a letter in your code. For example: C corresponds to P. Corresponding the alphabet to numbers, P = 16, so we rotate back 16 times, making it originally a Z.
